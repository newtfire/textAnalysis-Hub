Harry is a common given name, which has its origins in Germanic languages. It means "ruler of the home" or "army leader". There are many notable people throughout history who have been named Harry, including:

Harry Houdini, a Hungarian-American illusionist and stunt performer, who was known for his sensational escape acts. He was one of the most famous and successful magicians of his time.
Harry S. Truman, the 33rd President of the United States, who served from 1945 to 1953. He is best known for his role in the end of World War II and the dropping of atomic bombs on Japan, as well as the beginning of the Cold War.
Harry Styles, an English singer,songwriter, and actor. He rose to fame as a member of the boy band One Direction, and later launched a successful solo career.
Harry Connick Jr, an American singer, actor, pianist, and composer. He has sold over 28 million albums worldwide, and is known for his talents in jazz, pop, and big band music.
Harry H. Corbett, an English actor, best known for his portrayal of Harold Steptoe in the British television series Steptoe and Son
Harry Potter, a fictional character and the main protagonist of the Harry Potter series of novels written by J.K. Rowling. The Harry Potter series tells the story of a young wizard, Harry James Potter, and his friends Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, as they attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry and face various challenges, including the defeat of the Dark wizard, Lord Voldemort.
These are just a few examples of notable people named Harry throughout history, there are many more notable figures, athletes, actors, politicians and common people that have been named Harry.
